Abstract
Background: TDP-43 evolutionarily conserved RNA-binding protein implicated the
pathogenesis frontotemporal dementia (FTD), sporadic familial amyotrophic lateral sclerosis
(ALS), possibly neurodegenerative diseases. In diseased neurons, TDP-43 depleted in
nucleus, suggesting loss-of-function pathogenic mechanism. However, normal function of
TDP-43 postmitotic neurons largely unknown.
Results: Here demonstrate overexpression Drosophila TDP-43 (dTDP-43) vivo
significantly increases dendritic branching sensory neurons Drosophila larvae. Loss dTDP-43
function, genetic null mutant RNAi knockdown, decreased dendritic
branching. Further genetic analysis demonstrated cell-autonomous role dTDP-43 dendrite
formation. Moreover, human TDP-43 (hTDP-43) promoted dendritic branching Drosophila
neurons, function attenuated mutations associated ALS.
Conclusion: These findings reveal essential role TDP-43 dendritic structural integrity,
supporting notion loss normal TDP-43 function diseased neurons compromise
neuronal connectivity neuronal cell loss FTD ALS.
