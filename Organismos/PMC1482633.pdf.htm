Yeast tRNAHis guanylyltransferase, Thg1, is an essential protein
that adds a single guanine to the 5ⴕ end (Gⴚ1) of tRNAHis. This Gⴚ1
residue is required for aminoacylation of tRNAHis by histidyl-tRNA
synthetase, both in vitro and in vivo. The guanine nucleotide
addition reaction catalyzed by Thg1 extends the polynucleotide
chain in the reverse (3ⴕ-5ⴕ) direction of other known polymerases,
albeit by one nucleotide. Here, we show that alteration of the 3ⴕ
end of the Thg1 substrate tRNAHis unleashes an unexpected re-
verse polymerase activity of wild-type Thg1, resulting in the 3ⴕ-5ⴕ
addition of multiple nucleotides to the tRNA, with efficiency
comparable to the Gⴚ1 addition reaction. The addition of Gⴚ1 forms
a mismatched G䡠A base pair at the 5ⴕ end of tRNAHis, and, with
monophosphorylated tRNA substrates, it is absolutely specific for
