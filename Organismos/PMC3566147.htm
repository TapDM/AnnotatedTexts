Abstract
The Toll-like receptor (TLR)-mediated NF-kB pathway tightly controlled overactivation result severe
damage host, case chronic inflammatory diseases cancer. In mammals, sterile-alpha and
armadillo motif-containing protein (SARM) plays important role negatively regulating pathway. While
Caenorhabditis elegans SARM crucial efficient immune response bacterial fungal infections, still
unknown Drosophila SARM participates immune responses. Here, Litopenaeus vannamei SARM (LvSARM) was
cloned functionally characterized. LvSARM shared signature domains exhibited significant similarities to
mammalian SARM. Real-time quantitative PCR analysis indicated expression LvSARM responsive Vibrio
alginolyticus white spot syndrome virus (WSSV) infections hemocyte, gill, hepatopancreas intestine. In
Drosophila S2 cells, LvSARM widely distributed cytoplasm significantly inhibit promoters NF-
kB pathway-controlled antimicrobial peptide genes (AMPs). Silencing LvSARM using dsRNA-mediated RNA interference
increased expression levels Penaeidins antilipopolysaccharide factors, L.vannamei AMPs, increased
mortality rate V. alginolyticus infection. Taken together, results reveal LvSARM novel component
shrimp Toll pathway negatively regulates shrimp AMPs, particularly Penaeidins antilipopolysaccharide factors.
