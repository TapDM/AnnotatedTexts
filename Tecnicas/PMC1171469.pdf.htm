We also examined the secretion of periplasmic invertase
from the ts mutants (Figure 6D). Since our standard
strains lack the chromosomal invertase gene, they were
transformed with a plasmid which expresses a myc-tagged
version of the secreted form of invertase. Examination of
the intracellular invertase by immunoblotting showed that
there was a slight increase in the hyperglycosylated forms
of the enzyme in the mutant strains, suggesting a reduced
rate of transport through the Golgi to the cell surface. In
addition, the invertase present in the got1âˆ† sft2-1 strain
had slightly shorter polysaccharide chains than normal,
consistent with a reduction in mannosyltransferase activity
in the Golgi apparatus of this strain.
Total glycoprotein secretion was also examined in these
strains by incubating spheroplasts with 35S-labelled amino
acids and monitoring the appearance of labelled concanav-
alin A-binding glycoproteins in the medium (as described
in Holthuis et al., 1998b). No significant quantitative or
qualitative abnormalities were observed (data not shown).
