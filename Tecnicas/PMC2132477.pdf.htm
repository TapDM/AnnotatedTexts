This study addresses G1 functions of CDC37, but this
gene may also be important in other stages of the cell cy-
cle. The alleles discussed here were specifically selected
for their behavior at Start (Reed 1980a, b) and may not ad-
dress all the requirements for CDC37. Several of the pro-
tein kinases intimate with CDC37 perform functions later
in the cell cycle, raising the possibility that CDC37 could
also be important after G1. Mps1p participates in a spindle
assembly checkpoint in G2/M (Weiss and Winey, 1996),
and casein kinase II is also required around this time
(Hanna et al., 1995). Similarly, Cdc28p is required to pro-
mote cell cycle progression not only at Start, but also in
S phase and in G2/M (for review see Nasmyth, 1993; Fig. 9).
Gerber et al. (1995) report biochemical evidence for a
CDC37 requirement in G2/M: cdc37-1 mutant strains also
exhibit a defect in binding and activation of Cdc28p by the
mitotic cyclin Clb2p. The recent isolation of CDC37 alleles
that affect pp60v-src activity and cause a G2/M cell cycle ar-
rest provides genetic evidence to bolster this idea (Dey et
al., 1996b). A variety of alleles of this gene may be needed
to explore and understand the functions of CDC37 in G1
and throughout the cell cycle.
