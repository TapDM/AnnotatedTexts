Abstract
Background: The Prion protein (PRNP/Prp) plays crucial role transmissible spongiform encephalopathies (TSEs) like
Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD), scrapie mad cow disease. Notwithstanding importance human animal
disease, fundamental aspects PRNP/Prp function transmission remains unaccounted for.

Methodology/Principal Findings: The zebrafish (Danio rerio) genome contains Prp encoding genes assigned prp1,
prp2 prp3. Currently, second paralogue believed similar mammalian PRNP gene structure
function. Functional studies PRNP gene ortholog addressed prp2 morpholino (MO) knockdown
experiments. Investigation Prp2 depleted embryos revealed high mortality apoptosis 24 hours post fertilization
(hpf) impaired brain neuronal development. In order elucidate underlying mechanisms, genome-
wide transcriptome analysis carried viable 24 hpf morphants. The resulting changes gene expression profiles
revealed 249 differently expressed genes linked biological processes like cell death, neurogenesis embryonic
development.

Conclusions/Significance: The current study contributes understanding basic Prp functions demonstrates that
zebrafish excellent model address role Prp vertebrates. The gene knockdown prp2 indicates an
essential biological function zebrafish ortholog morphant phenotype suggests neurodegenerative
action gene expression effects apoptosis related effects gene networks controlling neurogenesis and
embryo development.
